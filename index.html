🗓️ Timeline

3-Week Training Timeline

This section remains presentation-ready and interactive, with hover-responsive timeline cards and smooth reveal animations while scrolling.

Week 1

Foundation & Insurance Basics

The first week focuses on orientation, insurance knowledge, terminology, and introducing the VA to systems and work expectations.

  • Company orientation and VA expectations
  • Insurance fundamentals and core terminology
  • Forms, declarations, and document types
  • Professional communication and documentation habits
Week 2

Tools, Process, and Guided Practice

The second week becomes more practical, using simulations, guided walkthroughs, and task-focused exercises to build confidence.

  • AMS and carrier system navigation
  • Task simulations and accuracy exercises
  • Email handling, follow-ups, and checklist usage
  • Scenario-based quoting and process walkthroughs
Week 3

Confidence Building & Client Readiness

The final week focuses on quality control, shadowing, review, and preparing the VA to move smoothly into live client support.

  • Advanced practice and quality control
  • Shadowing and transition preparation
  • Performance review and coaching
  • Readiness for client-endorsed workflow support
💼 Section 03

Working with the Client

Once the VA completes the classroom-based training, the next phase becomes more client-focused. At this stage, there is little to no classroom discussion, and the VA learns more directly through the client’s actual workflow, systems, standards, and daily task expectations.

Transition Phase

The VA moves from structured internal discussion into a more realistic working setup with greater exposure to live tasks and real processes.

Client-Led Growth

The client may continue guiding the VA based on preferred workflow, business priorities, and the specific process used in their daily operation.

Final Stage

Operational Readiness

LAVA
  • Reduced classroom discussion and increased real-work exposure
  • Client onboarding into specific tasks, systems, and expectations
  • Performance is observed through actual workflow participation
  • Continuous learning through direct communication and feedback
  • Goal is independence, confidence, and ownership of tasks

Outcome

By the end of this phase, the VA is expected to support the client with less dependence on classroom training and stronger confidence in handling tasks according to the client’s real workflow.
